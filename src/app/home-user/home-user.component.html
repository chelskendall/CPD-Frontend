Introduction to Professional Registration and Continuous Professional Development (CPD)

Professional Registration Your Path to Recognition and Expertise

Professional Registration within engineering refers to the process by which engineers obtain formal recognition of their qualifications and experience, typically through a professional engineering organization or regulatory body.
This recognition demonstrates that an engineer has met specific educational, ethical, and competency standards, and is qualified to practice engineering in a particular jurisdiction.

 Continuous Professional Development (CPD) Transforming Potential into Progress

A definition of CPD commonly accepted by The Engineering Council’s member institutions is “The systematic maintenance, improvement and broadening of knowledge and skill, and the development of personal qualities necessary for the execution of professional and technical duties throughout the practitioner’s working life.”
Continuing means one needs to commit oneself to a lifelong learning process and self-development.

Initiating the CPD process commences with conducting an honest self-assessment and defining professional development objectives. The subsequent step involves creating a strategic action plan aimed at attaining these objectives. Once the CPD plan is established, it's time to actively engage in your professional development activities.

Registration certification by the Board of Engineering of Trinidad and Tobago (BOETT) means an obligation and accountability to conform to a legislated code of ethics and to protect public health, safety, and welfare; assessment, validation, and commitment of the engineer to demonstrate competence, objectivity, and expertise; and lastly, confidence and trust to meet recognized academic and professional standards.

  • Applicants for registration must satisfy that their Bachelor’s degree certificates are recognized and accredited and that they have at least four years of engineering experience after graduation.

  • All eligible persons must provide evidence of “further learning” through a recognized Master’s degree or an accumulation of similar type academic knowledge.

  • An application form must be downloaded, completed, and returned together with a TTD$200.00 application fee.

  • Persons should also provide acceptable supporting evidence of qualifications and professional organization memberships (originals and one copy each) as well as statements from two sponsors, duly signed and dated, attesting to specifics of tasks performed that would now enable the applicant to undertake, on their own, engineering work of a professional standard.

Their core purpose is to set and maintain the internationally recognized standards for the UK engineering profession, found in the UK Standard for Professional Engineering Competence (UK-SPEC) and the ICTTech Standard. Chartered Engineers (CEng) develop solutions to engineering problems using new or existing technologies, through innovation, creativity and change and/or they may have technical accountability for complex systems with significant levels of risk.

  • Engineers wishing to apply for registration are first required to join a professional engineering institution or a professional affiliate of the Engineering Council, licensed to assess candidates.

  • They must then inform their institution of their aim for professional registration, so that the institute can advise on title eligibility and finding a mentor.

  • Applicants need to record their professional development, and evidence on qualifications and experiences.

  • The assessment process starts with a written application, and once approved, the institution will submit a registration form to the Engineering Council on the candidate’s behalf.
